Understanding MLC Medical Requirements

Maritime Labour Convention (MLC 2006) establishes global standards for seafarer medical fitness

General MLC Certificate

Basic medical certificate issued by doctors approved by the country's maritime administration. Valid for 2 years (1 year for seafarers under 18).

  • • Internationally recognized under MLC 2006
  • • Required for all seafarers regardless of flag
  • • Issued by nationally approved doctors

Flag-Specific Requirements

Additional medical certificates required by specific flag states, often with their own approved doctors and forms.

  • • Flag state approved medical examiners
  • • Specific forms and procedures
  • • May override general MLC requirements

Important Note

While MLC certificates should be internationally recognized, many flag states and port state control inspectors prefer their own specific formats. Always verify requirements with the relevant flag administration.

Frequently Asked Questions

While MLC certificates should be internationally recognized, many flag states have additional requirements. Some flags require specific forms, approved doctors, or additional validations. Always check with the flag administration before proceeding.
If your certificate is rejected, you may need to get a new examination from an approved doctor or complete additional forms required by the flag state. Some flags offer transfer or validation processes for existing certificates.
Check the official flag administration websites or contact them directly. Many flags publish lists of approved medical examiners. You can also find links in our Forms & Links section above.
